Beacon's End is a top-down arcade strategy video game developed by me and six of my peers as the final project of my Game Development Minor. The game takes place in the ocean where each night you have a quota of ships you need to bring to harbor before the local Lovecraftian Monster (named "Benthylavig the Deep Devourer" (or "Benny" for short)) wakes up and destroys your lighthouse. The full game takes place over five night, each night introducing more ship types, different obstacles, and a larger quota.

As lead developer, I developed the tutorial, dialogue system, level transitions, rock placements, ship spawn rate and behavior, and the majority of the level design. The other three developers programmed the monster behavior, monster animations, ship effects, rock destruction, UI, start screen, credit screen, end screens, background effects, hitboxes and the other one hundred things that go into making a video game.

![Level Transition GIF](/assets/img/Programming/beacons-end_transition.gif)
*GIF of the Level Transition animation*

I had a really fun time developing Beacons End. Some of the most fun I've ever had doing programming actually. There's always another problem, another system you want to develop, a mechanic that doesn't feel right, something. But the work is satisfying, and watching people play and have fun playing our game was all worth it in the end.
